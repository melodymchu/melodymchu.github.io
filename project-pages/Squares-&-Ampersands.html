Squares and Ampersands: Form and Composition Project

September 2022

For my Communication Design Fundamentals class, we were given a list of word pairs to illustrate using only solid black squares and ampersands (&). The word pairs were: Playful/Serious, Noisy/Quiet, and Dangerous/Safe. Through sketching 30 thumbnails, digitizing in Adobe Illustrator, receiving feedback, and iterating, we developed the final designs.

Digital Design

Adobe Illustrator

Ideation Phase - Initial Sketches

During the initial sketching phase, I focused on generating as many ideas as possible to visually convey the given words. It was challenging to create 30 sketches, and while some were more successful than others, each idea offered a unique approach to the word pairings. This process allowed me to explore a wide range of concepts and choose the most effective designs to move forward with.

Digital Iterations

After receiving feedback on my sketches, I digitized my selected designs in Adobe Illustrator. This was my first experience with Illustrator, where I learned the basics of translating, rotating, and scaling objects, as well as using artboards, layers, and clipping masks. These digital iterations helped refine the designs further, considering both visual aesthetics and the principles of form and composition.

Final Result

The final designs effectively illustrate the word pairs using only black squares and ampersands. This project allowed me to practice applying design principles, receive and incorporate feedback, and gain experience with Adobe Illustrator. The constraints of using a limited set of elements forced me to think creatively and iterate my designs multiple times, refining the visual communication of each concept.

Reflection

Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed this project. It was a valuable exercise in creative problem-solving within specific constraints, and it enhanced my understanding of design principles like Gestalt. The iterative process and critiques helped refine my ideas, demonstrating the importance of feedback in design. I look forward to applying these lessons and skills in future projects.
