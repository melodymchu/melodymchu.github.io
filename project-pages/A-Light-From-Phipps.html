A Light From Phipps

March - April 2024

Inspired by the iconic local Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens, I designed a floor lamp as an homage to its mission to the public, natural elements, and Victorian style, blending organic forms with architectural elements and materials.

Project Goals

Assignment: Design a commercial product that captures the essence of a place in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.


  • Collaborative Research: Conducted group research to analyze market trends and major competitors, examining their unique processes, technologies, and target audiences.
  • Market Analysis: Identified key market players and explored their manufacturing methods, design philosophies, and target demographics.
  • Target Market Insight: Investigated the target markets for competing products, understanding their needs and purchasing behaviors.
  • Individual Research: Performed in-person research by visiting and photographing the chosen Pittsburgh location, capturing its unique characteristics and form language to inform the design.
  • Design Integration: Applied research findings, including insights from the location photos, to create a product that embodies the essence of the Pittsburgh site. Reflected user needs and company goals while incorporating distinct form language elements.
  • Form Language Exploration: Utilized form language principles to develop and refine the product’s visual identity and aesthetics, ensuring alignment with the project’s thematic inspiration.
  • Visual Communication: Produced detailed design deliverables, including 2D dimensional drawings, sketches, and visual representations of the product.
  • Presentation and Project Management: Prepared and delivered effective presentations showcasing the project’s progress and final design. Managed both group and individual tasks efficiently, ensuring timely completion and organization of research and design documentation.

Research: identify and understand the problem space.



Research

The design process began with a comprehensive study of the Phipps Conservatory's imagery and history. By analyzing the conservatory's architecture and botanical themes, I developed a concept for a lamp that incorporates both organic and architectural forms. This inspiration guided the initial sketches and CAD models, blending natural curves with structured design elements to create a visually harmonious product.

Final Design

The final lamp design captures the essence of the Phipps Conservatory with its elegant form and thoughtful use of materials. The lamp's base and shade mimic the conservatory's glass and steel structure, while the organic curves reflect the natural beauty of the plants housed within. The use of sustainable materials and LED technology further enhances the design, aligning with modern trends in environmentally conscious product design.

Final Presentation

The project culminated in a final presentation, where I showcased the design process, research insights, and the final product. The presentation highlighted the integration of architectural and natural elements, as well as the product's potential appeal in the current market. Feedback from peers and instructors emphasized the project's strong conceptual foundation and effective execution.

Reflection

This project was a valuable experience in blending artistic inspiration with practical design considerations. By capturing the essence of a well-known Pittsburgh landmark in a commercial product, I learned the importance of thorough research, iterative design, and effective visual communication. These lessons will inform my future projects, as I continue to explore the intersection of design, architecture, and nature.
