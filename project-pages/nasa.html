NASA Internship Merch Designs

Summer 2020

During my internships at NASA Goddard, I conceptualized and designed the official summer intern t-shirt. I also created a design for the NASA Pathways Program patch, inspired by the solar eclipse, the iconic NASA logo, and symbols of innovation, such as a lightbulb, representing bright ideas and the future of NASA. This project allowed me to blend creative design with NASA’s storied legacy, aiming to create visuals that inspire interns and celebrate their journey with NASA.

Summer Intern T-Shirt Design

I designed a bold, playful t-shirt for NASA Goddard’s 2021 summer interns, with the iconic NASA wordmark logo tilted diagonally across the entire front, adding a sense of movement and energy. Hanging from the logo is a small red Mars rover, bringing a touch of whimsy and showcasing NASA's exploratory spirit. Each sleeve features a patch representing the intern programs, creating a sense of unity and pride among interns. On the back, the classic NASA globe logo is filled with the names of all 2021 Goddard interns, representing their contributions and connection to NASA’s mission. "NASA Goddard Interns 2021" is printed boldly across the back, emphasizing the significance of their shared experience. With a color palette of blue, white, and red to align with the NASA logo, the design captures the pride, playfulness, and team spirit of being a NASA intern.

I created a lively, playful t-shirt design concept for the NASA Goddard 2021 summer interns, featuring the iconic NASA wordmark logo tilted diagonally across the front to give it a sense of movement. A cute red Mars rover was intended to hang off the logo, with its arm extended for a more dynamic look, and patches on each sleeve represented the intern programs. The back design included the classic NASA globe logo filled with the names of all the 2021 interns, alongside a bold "NASA Goddard Interns 2021" to celebrate their shared experience. While the final implementation wasn’t exactly as I envisioned—some adjustments were made, including the rover’s arm position and a more static angle for the wordmark—it was still an honor to have my design and concept chosen for the official shirt.
